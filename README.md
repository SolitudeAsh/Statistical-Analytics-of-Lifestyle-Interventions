This cumulative data science project utilizes an RMarkdown framework to conduct a comprehensive statistical evaluation of a lifestyle intervention study comprising 1,500 adult participants. The primary objective of the research is to assess the individual and combined effects of randomized dietary structures—including Low Carb, Mediterranean, and Intermittent Fasting plans—and exercise regimens, which span Cardio, Strength, and Mixed modalities. Using a dataset that tracks diverse anthropometric, metabolic, cardiovascular, and behavioral variables over time, the project rigorously evaluates how these various interventions influence physical health, cognitive responses, and participant satisfaction. The analytical lifecycle begins with baseline continuous data tracking to establish the foundational characteristics of the cohort and determine whether the interventions yield significant immediate changes in metrics like weight loss, fitness scores, and cholesterol levels. As the data structures expand into categorical distributions, the project applies parametric and non-parametric techniques to investigate specific behavioral trends. This includes conducting uniform distribution checks on psychological satisfaction ratings, verifying single-proportion target goals, and mapping the independence of programmatic milestones across distinct geographical gym locations. In its advanced phases, the scope transitions into complex relational testing to discover variations in performance indicators across demographic groups and treatment categories. Through a sequential application of Two-Sample t-tests, One-Way ANOVAs, Tukey’s HSD, and robust non-parametric alternatives like the Kruskal-Wallis test, the project investigates group differences and addresses critical violations of standard statistical assumptions. Ultimately, the study concludes with multi-factor evaluations that isolate the primary impacts and interaction effects of combined interventions, providing a data-driven understanding of how integrated lifestyle changes shape holistic human health.
